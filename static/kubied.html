Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) Models

Designed by Allan, Stella, Martha, Isaiah, Michael

Waterfall Model

The Waterfall Model is a linear sequential flow where progress is seen as flowing steadily downwards (like a waterfall) through phases.

Agile Model

The Agile Model emphasizes iterative development and collaboration between self-organizing cross-functional teams.

V-Model

The V-Model, or Validation and Verification Model, extends the waterfall model by emphasizing the importance of testing at each development stage.

Iterative Model

The Iterative Model focuses on repetition of phases and refinement of deliverables through multiple iterations.

Spiral Model

The Spiral Model combines iterative development with the systematic aspects of the waterfall model, emphasizing risk assessment.
